What is marketing?

Many people think that marketing is the same thing as selling. However, it is much broader. Marketing covers many areas of your business, including:

  • how your customers are greeted on the phone
  • your customer service procedures
  • what your staff wear
  • the signature you use on your business emails.

Marketing consists of a mix of activities that build your brand and business. It’s about identifying and understanding your customers and developing products and services that meet their needs.

By investing in marketing, you can grow your business through:

  • attracting new customers
  • focusing on meeting your customers’ needs
  • building lasting relationships with your customers.

The 5 Ps of marketing

Working through the 5 Ps of marketing can help you build your brand and business in a consistent way. Each of the Ps will help you think about which areas you can change or improve to meet the needs of your market.

The five Ps are:

  1. Product – What are you selling? What makes your product different?
  2. Price – How do you set the price for your product or service?
  3. Promotion – How do you tell people about your business and products?
  4. Place – How and where do you deliver your product or service - shopfront, online or a distributor?
  5. People – How do you give customer service to your customers?

Why you need a marketing plan?

Having a marketing plan will help you better understand your business, your customers and your competitors. Through the process of planning and researching you can:

  • identify your target market and understand how your product or service can meet their needs
  • identify your competitors and their strengths and weaknesses
  • find ways to position your brand, products and services to stand out from your competitors
  • set specific, measurable goals and timeframes for your marketing activities
  • map out a marketing strategy to reach your target audience, including the messages, channels and tools you will use.

Writing your marketing plan

Your marketing plan should be tailored to your business and what you need. Things you may have in your marketing plan include:

  • analysis of your market, industry and trends
  • your key marketing strategies
  • steps to achieve your business objectives, including marketing activities you plan to use
  • your proposed budget for marketing
  • a timeline of when you will implement your planned activities
  • your marketing goals.

It's also important to make sure that you continue to develop and update your marketing plan as your business grows and changes.
