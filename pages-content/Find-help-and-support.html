ATO app

The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) has a free ATO app to make it easier for you to conduct your tax and super affairs on the go.

Through the app, you can:

  • calculate the amount of tax to withhold from employee salary and wage payments
  • check an Australian business number (ABN) using ABN Lookup
  • set important due date reminders and alerts for tax and super obligations
  • monitor your financial health with the business performance check tool.

Sole traders can also use the ATO app to enrol their voiceprint for quick and easy access to the ATO’s online services for individuals and sole traders.

Australian Tax Office

 ATO Small business assist

The ATO’s Small business assist provides easy access to information from a range of government websites. Simply type in a question and Small business assist will provide tailored information for you, including:

  • understanding and registering for GST
  • your responsibilities as an employer
  • lodging activity statements.

Small business assist also offers support services to help you get in touch with the ATO:

  • Search 'after hours call back' to book an after-hours call back from the ATO.
  • Use 'live chat support' to chat online with the ATO.

Finding a tax practitioner

A tax practitioner can help you prepare or lodge your tax returns, notices or statements, or provide you with tax advice.

Tax practitioners include:

  • tax agents
  • BAS agents
  • tax (financial) advisers.

Before accepting the services of a tax practitioner, make sure they are registered with the Tax Practitioners Board (TPB).

You can check to see if a tax practitioner is registered by:

  • looking for the registered tax practitioner symbol
  • checking the TPB Register.

Did you know?

Financial advisers who provide tax (financial) advice services for a fee or other reward also need to be registered.

Legal help for employers

The Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO) provides free services to help employers with their legal obligations in the workplace.

Check out the FWO’s information for small business for help on:

  • getting started – the basics, hiring employees and registering for an account with FWO
  • employment related record-keeping
  • finding the right pay
  • calculating leave
  • managing employees
  • ending employment
  • managing payroll integrity.

The FWO also has a Small Business Helpline on 13 13 94 to help you with any questions you may have on your workplace relations obligations.
