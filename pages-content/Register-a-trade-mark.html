Benefits of a trade mark

A trade mark protects a particular type of intellectual property (IP). It can be a unique symbol, word(s), sound, number, image or scent used to represent a business or its products.

Once registered, a trade mark is an asset that gives you the sole right to use, license or sell it to others.

Before registering a trade mark

To be eligible to register a trade mark you must:

  • apply as an individual business owner and not under your business name
  • be an individual, company, incorporated association or a combination of these
  • use, or plan to use the trade mark

Before you register, search IP Australia's Australian Trade Marks Search System to make sure the trade mark you want to use is available.

You can apply for your trade mark through the IP Australia website. Before you register for a trade mark make sure you know:

  • what goods and services to protect
  • the type of trade mark you need
  • if your trade mark is available

Trade mark costs

The cost of applying for a trade mark will depend on the protection you’re after.

Products and services are grouped together in classes and fees apply to each class. For example, clothing, footwear and headgear are under one class, whereas software is in a different class.

If you're trying to apply for a trade mark in more than one class, you'll need to pay more.

Timeframe for registering a trade mark

IP Australia usually take three to four months to examine your trade mark application once it has been submitted.

If your trade mark is accepted you’ll be notified in writing and your trade mark will then be advertised as an accepted application in the Australian Official Journal of Trade Marks.

Head to the IP Australia website for more information on the timeframes of application outcomes. 

Understand the legal requirements to register a trade mark

There is no legal requirement to register a trade mark.

Renew your trade mark

A trade mark is protected in all Australian states and territories for an initial period of 10 years. You can renew your trade mark registration 12 months before the renewal date or up to 6 months after.

If you renew after the due date you may have to pay a late fee. 

Understand the difference between a TM and ® symbol

You can use the ™ symbol alongside an unregistered trade mark. It lets others know that you’re using the logo, image, word(s), sound, number or scent as a trade mark.

The ® symbol shows that you’ve registered the trade mark and you have exclusive rights to the mark. It’s an offence to use the ® symbol for unregistered trade marks. (449)
